Component Formulation References#

This page lists academic references that directly support PetraFlow component formulations. The focus is on papers that introduce, systematize, or strengthen mathematical models; software-framework and documentation-layout references are intentionally omitted here.

Power Flow, OPF, and Convex Relaxations#

Modeling topic

Representative reference

Related components

Original OPF problem

Carpentier, “Contribution a l’etude du dispatching economique”, Bulletin de la Societe Francaise des Electriciens, 1962

OPF problem and economic-dispatch objective

SDP OPF relaxation

Lavaei and Low, “Zero Duality Gap in Optimal Power Flow Problem”, IEEE Transactions on Power Systems, 27(1), 92-107, 2012

WSDPPowerFlow, \(W\succeq0\), rank-1 relaxation

Mesh-network SDP relaxation

Madani, Sojoudi, and Lavaei, “Convex Relaxation for Optimal Power Flow Problem: Mesh Networks”, IEEE Transactions on Power Systems, 30(1), 199-211, 2015

mesh ACOPF SDP and chordal SDP ideas

Large-scale SDP OPF

Molzahn, Holzer, Lesieutre, and DeMarco, “Implementation of a Large-Scale Optimal Power Flow Solver Based on Semidefinite Programming”, IEEE Transactions on Power Systems, 28(4), 3987-3998, 2013

clique/chordal decomposition and large-scale SDP implementation ideas

SOC/SDP equivalence

Low, “Convex Relaxation of Optimal Power Flow-Part I: Formulations and Equivalence”, IEEE Transactions on Control of Network Systems, 1(1), 15-27, 2014

W-space, branch-flow, SOC/SDP relationships

Distributed SDP OPF

Dall’Anese, Zhu, and Giannakis, “Distributed Optimal Power Flow for Smart Microgrids”, IEEE Transactions on Smart Grid, 4(3), 1464-1475, 2013

microgrid/distribution SDP relaxation and distributed OPF

QC relaxation

Coffrin, Hijazi, and Van Hentenryck, “The QC Relaxation: A Theoretical and Computational Study on Optimal Power Flow”, IEEE Transactions on Power Systems, 31(4), 3008-3018, 2016

QCPowerFlowRelaxation, voltage-product and trigonometric envelopes

Strengthened SDP/QC

Coffrin, Hijazi, and Van Hentenryck, “Strengthening the SDP Relaxation of AC Power Flows With Convex Envelopes, Bound Tightening, and Valid Inequalities”, IEEE Transactions on Power Systems, 32(5), 3549-3558, 2017

valid inequalities and bound tightening

Branch-flow SOCP

Farivar and Low, “Branch Flow Model: Relaxations and Convexification-Part I”, IEEE Transactions on Power Systems, 28(3), 2554-2564, 2013

BranchFlowSOCPowerFlow, \(P_{ij}^2+Q_{ij}^2\le v_i\ell_{ij}\)

DistFlow / LinDistFlow

Baran and Wu, “Optimal Capacitor Placement on Radial Distribution Systems”, IEEE Transactions on Power Delivery, 4(1), 725-734, 1989

LinDistFlowPowerFlow, radial distribution voltage-drop model

Storage, Unit Commitment, and Security#

Modeling topic

Representative reference

Related components

Storage scheduling

Harsha and Dahleh, “Optimal Management and Sizing of Energy Storage Under Dynamic Pricing for the Efficient Integration of Renewable Energy”, IEEE Transactions on Power Systems, 30(3), 1164-1181, 2015

StorageContinuous, charging/discharging power, energy state, efficiency

End-user storage

van de Ven, Hegde, Massoulie, and Salonidis, “Optimal Control of End-User Energy Storage”, IEEE Transactions on Smart Grid, 4(2), 789-797, 2013

storage energy balance and price-responsive dispatch

Distribution storage SOCP OPF

Grover-Silva, Girard, and Kariniotakis, “Optimal sizing and placement of distribution grid connected battery systems through an SOCP optimal power flow algorithm”, Applied Energy, 219, 385-393, 2018

storage siting, sizing, time-coupled dispatch, and branch-flow/SOCP distribution constraints

Batteries in renewable scheduling

Li, Uckun, Constantinescu, Birge, Hedman, and Botterud, “Flexible Operation of Batteries in Power System Scheduling With Renewable Energy”, IEEE Transactions on Sustainable Energy, 7(2), 685-696, 2016

storage coupled with stochastic UC and economic dispatch

Tight UC MILP

Morales-Espana, Latorre, and Ramos, “Tight and Compact MILP Formulation for the Thermal Unit Commitment Problem”, IEEE Transactions on Power Systems, 28(4), 4897-4908, 2013

UnitCommitment3BinStd, min up/down, ramp strengthening

Multi-service UC LP/MILP

Zhang, Capuder, and Mancarella, “Unified Unit Commitment Formulation and Fast Multi-Service LP Model for Flexibility Evaluation in Sustainable Power Systems”, IEEE Transactions on Sustainable Energy, 7(2), 658-671, 2016

multi-service scheduling, flexibility evaluation, and storage/UC coupling

SCOPF survey

Capitanescu et al., “State-of-the-Art, Challenges, and Future Trends in Security Constrained Optimal Power Flow”, Electric Power Systems Research, 81(8), 1731-1741, 2011

ContingencyConstraints, CorrectiveRedispatch

Chance-constrained OPF

Bienstock, Chertkov, and Harnett, “Chance-Constrained Optimal Power Flow: Risk-Aware Network Control under Uncertainty”, SIAM Review, 56(3), 461-495, 2014

uncertainty-aware security constraints and risk-aware OPF

Practical Advice#

  1. For exact ACOPF, compare ACPPowerFlow, ACRPowerFlow, and IVRPowerFlow on the same case.

  2. For convex relaxations, understand model strength and scale through WSDPPowerFlow, WSOCPowerFlow, BranchFlowSOCPowerFlow, and QCPowerFlowRelaxation.

  3. For hybrid AC/DC cases, use PGLIB-HVDC/MatACDC data and set network_type = :acdc explicitly.
