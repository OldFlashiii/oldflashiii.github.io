Power-Flow Formulations#

This page documents the main variables, constraints, and references behind PetraFlow power-flow components. The notation follows the MATPOWER, PowerModels, and OPF literature; init_power! and the component registry map these symbols to JuMP variables.

Notation#

Symbol

Meaning

\(\mathcal{N}\)

AC buses

\(\mathcal{E}\)

directed AC branches, \(k=(i,j)\)

\(\mathcal{G}_i\)

generators at bus \(i\)

\(V_i\)

complex voltage, \(V_i = V_i^m e^{j\theta_i} = e_i + j f_i\)

\(Y_{ff},Y_{ft},Y_{tf},Y_{tt}\)

branch admittance blocks including tap, phase shift, and charging

\(S_{ij}=P_{ij}+jQ_{ij}\)

complex branch power from \(i\) to \(j\)

\(P_i^d,Q_i^d\)

bus demand

\(P_g,Q_g\)

generator output

\(W_{ij}\)

voltage product, \(W_{ij}=V_iV_j^*\)

\(v_i,\ell_{ij}\)

squared voltage magnitude and squared current magnitude

The default bus balance is:

\[ \sum_{g\in\mathcal{G}_i} P_g + P_i^{dev} - P_i^d - \sum_{(i,j)\in\delta_i^+} P_{ij} - \sum_{(j,i)\in\delta_i^-} P_{ji} = 0 \]
\[ \sum_{g\in\mathcal{G}_i} Q_g + Q_i^{dev} - Q_i^d - \sum_{(i,j)\in\delta_i^+} Q_{ij} - \sum_{(j,i)\in\delta_i^-} Q_{ji} = 0 \]

ACPPowerFlow: polar AC power flow#

init_power!(ctx; formulation = :acp, state_variables = :polar)
add_modules!(ctx, ACPPowerFlow())

Variables:

\[ V_i^m,\theta_i,P_{ij},Q_{ij},P_g,Q_g \]

Branch current and power:

\[ I_{ij}=Y_{ff}V_i+Y_{ft}V_j,\qquad S_{ij}=V_i I_{ij}^* \]

Expanded polar branch flow:

\[ P_{ij} =G_{ff}(V_i^m)^2 +V_i^mV_j^m\left(G_{ft}\cos(\theta_i-\theta_j)+B_{ft}\sin(\theta_i-\theta_j)\right) \]
\[ Q_{ij} =-B_{ff}(V_i^m)^2 +V_i^mV_j^m\left(G_{ft}\sin(\theta_i-\theta_j)-B_{ft}\cos(\theta_i-\theta_j)\right) \]

Typical constraints:

\[ V_i^{min}\le V_i^m\le V_i^{max},\qquad \theta_{ref}=0 \]
\[ P_{ij}^2+Q_{ij}^2\le (S_{ij}^{max})^2 \]

Class: NLP.

ACRPowerFlow: rectangular AC power flow#

init_power!(ctx; formulation = :acr, state_variables = :rectangular)
add_modules!(ctx, ACRPowerFlow())

Variables:

\[ e_i,f_i,P_{ij},Q_{ij},P_g,Q_g \]

With \(V_i=e_i+jf_i\):

\[ I_{ij}=Y_{ff}V_i+Y_{ft}V_j=I_{ij}^r+jI_{ij}^i \]
\[ P_{ij}=e_iI_{ij}^r+f_iI_{ij}^i,\qquad Q_{ij}=f_iI_{ij}^r-e_iI_{ij}^i \]
\[ (V_i^{min})^2\le e_i^2+f_i^2\le (V_i^{max})^2 \]

Class: NLP.

IVRPowerFlow: current-voltage AC power flow#

init_power!(ctx; formulation = :ivr, state_variables = :ivr)
add_modules!(ctx, IVRPowerFlow())

Variables:

\[ e_i,f_i,I_{ij}^r,I_{ij}^i,P_{ij},Q_{ij},P_g,Q_g \]

Branch current is linear in rectangular voltages:

\[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} I_{ij}^r\\ I_{ij}^i \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \Re(Y_{ff}) & -\Im(Y_{ff}) & \Re(Y_{ft}) & -\Im(Y_{ft})\\ \Im(Y_{ff}) & \Re(Y_{ff}) & \Im(Y_{ft}) & \Re(Y_{ft}) \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} e_i\\ f_i\\ e_j\\ f_j \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

Power coupling remains bilinear:

\[ P_{ij}=e_iI_{ij}^r+f_iI_{ij}^i,\qquad Q_{ij}=f_iI_{ij}^r-e_iI_{ij}^i \]

Class: NLP.

DCPPowerFlow: DC B-theta power flow#

init_power!(ctx; formulation = :dcp, state_variables = :dc)
add_modules!(ctx, DCPPowerFlow())

Variables:

\[ \theta_i,P_{ij},P_g \]

Under the standard lossless DC assumptions:

\[ P_{ij}=b_{ij}\left(\theta_i-\theta_j-\theta_{ij}^{shift}\right) \]
\[ \sum_{i\in\mathcal{N}}\left(\sum_{g\in\mathcal{G}_i}P_g-P_i^d+P_i^{dev}\right)=0 \]

Class: LP or QP when quadratic generation cost is used.

PTDFPowerFlow#

Variables:

\[ p_i^{net},P_k,P_g \]
\[ p_i^{net}=\sum_{g\in\mathcal{G}_i}P_g-P_i^d+P_i^{dev} \]
\[ \sum_i p_i^{net}=0,\qquad P_k=\sum_{i\in\mathcal{N}}\mathrm{PTDF}_{k,i}p_i^{net}+P_k^0 \]

Class: LP/QP.

WSOCPowerFlow: W-space SOC relaxation#

init_power!(ctx; formulation = :w_soc, state_variables = :w_space)
add_modules!(ctx, WSOCPowerFlow())

Variables:

\[ W_{ii},W_{ij}^r,W_{ij}^i,P_{ij},Q_{ij} \]
\[ W_{ii}=|V_i|^2,\qquad W_{ij}=V_iV_j^*=W_{ij}^r+jW_{ij}^i \]
\[ P_{ij}=G_{ff}W_{ii}+G_{ft}W_{ij}^r+B_{ft}W_{ij}^i \]
\[ Q_{ij}=-B_{ff}W_{ii}+G_{ft}W_{ij}^i-B_{ft}W_{ij}^r \]
\[ (W_{ij}^r)^2+(W_{ij}^i)^2\le W_{ii}W_{jj} \]

Class: SOCP.

WSDPPowerFlow: W-space SDP relaxation#

The lifted voltage matrix satisfies:

\[ W=VV^H,\qquad W\succeq0,\qquad (V_i^{min})^2\le W_{ii}\le (V_i^{max})^2 \]

The original AC model also requires:

\[ \mathrm{rank}(W)=1 \]

The SDP relaxation drops the rank constraint. Class: SDP.

QCPowerFlowRelaxation#

QC relaxations use convex envelopes for voltage products, trigonometric terms, and bilinear products. A typical McCormick envelope is:

\[ \underline{x}y+\underline{y}x-\underline{x}\underline{y} \le xy \le \overline{x}y+\underline{y}x-\overline{x}\underline{y} \]
\[ \underline{x}y+\overline{y}x-\underline{x}\overline{y} \le xy \le \overline{x}y+\overline{y}x-\overline{x}\overline{y} \]

Class: SOCP/QCQP.

BranchFlowSOCPowerFlow#

init_power!(ctx; formulation = :bf_soc, state_variables = :voltage_square)
add_modules!(ctx, BranchFlowSOCPowerFlow())

Variables:

\[ P_{ij},Q_{ij},v_i,\ell_{ij} \]

DistFlow voltage drop:

\[ v_j=v_i-2(r_{ij}P_{ij}+x_{ij}Q_{ij}) +(r_{ij}^2+x_{ij}^2)\ell_{ij} \]

SOC current relation:

\[ P_{ij}^2+Q_{ij}^2\le v_i\ell_{ij} \]

LinDistFlowPowerFlow removes losses and current-square variables:

\[ v_j=v_i-2(r_{ij}P_{ij}+x_{ij}Q_{ij}) \]

Class: SOCP or LP.
